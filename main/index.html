About Our Church

Our History

Founded in 1975, the Church of Christ at High Level Makurdi has been a beacon of hope and faith in our community for nearly five decades. We started as a small gathering of believers meeting in a rented space and have grown by God's grace to become one of the most vibrant congregations in the region.

Our journey has been marked by faithful service, biblical teaching, and a commitment to spreading the Gospel in its purest form. We remain devoted to the restoration principles that characterized the early church.

Our Vision

To be a Christ-centered community that transforms lives through the power of the Gospel, equipping believers to impact their world for God's kingdom.

Our Mission

To proclaim Jesus Christ through biblical teaching, authentic worship, compassionate service, and intentional discipleship that leads to spiritual growth.

Our Values

Biblical Authority • Prayer • Discipleship • Community • Service • Evangelism • Unity in Christ • Transformation

Our Core Beliefs

The Bible

We believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God, the only infallible rule of faith and practice for Christians. It is complete and sufficient for teaching, reproof, correction, and training in righteousness (2 Timothy 3:16-17).

Jesus Christ

We believe in Jesus Christ as the Son of God, our only Savior and Lord. Through His death and resurrection, we have salvation and the promise of eternal life. He is the head of the church and our mediator with God (1 Timothy 2:5).

Salvation & Baptism

We believe salvation comes by grace through faith (Ephesians 2:8-9), expressed through repentance and baptism by immersion for the forgiveness of sins (Acts 2:38). Baptism is our response to God's grace, not a work of merit but an act of faith.

The Church

We believe the church is the body of Christ on earth, established by Jesus and built upon the confession that He is the Christ (Matthew 16:18). The church is autonomous in its governance, following the New Testament pattern for organization and worship.

Communion

We observe the Lord's Supper every first day of the week as a memorial of Christ's sacrifice (Acts 20:7). The bread represents His body, and the cup represents His blood shed for our redemption.

Christian Living

We believe Christians are called to live holy lives, distinct from the world (Romans 12:1-2). This includes loving God with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength, and loving our neighbors as ourselves (Mark 12:30-31).

Christ's Return

We believe in the personal, imminent return of Jesus Christ, who will judge the living and the dead (2 Timothy 4:1). He will receive believers unto Himself and establish His eternal kingdom (1 Thessalonians 4:16-17).
